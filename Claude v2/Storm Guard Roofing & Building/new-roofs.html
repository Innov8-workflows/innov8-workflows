What We Do

Full Re-Roofs That Last

Sometimes a roof reaches the end of its life. Widespread slipped or cracked tiles, a sagging roofline, recurring leaks that keep coming back no matter how many repairs are done — these are signs that patching up isn't the answer any more. Storm Guard Roofing & Building carries out complete re-roofs across Dundee, Perth and Angus, giving your home a fresh start with quality materials and proper workmanship throughout.

With over 10 years' experience on Scottish roofs, we know exactly what these buildings demand. We'll survey your existing roof, talk you through your options honestly, and give you a clear fixed quote before any work starts.

When Does a Roof Need Replacing?

Not every tired roof needs a full strip — sometimes a well-targeted roof repair is all it takes, and we'll always tell you if that's the case. But if you're seeing multiple problem areas, large areas of cracked or porous tiles, a mortar bed that's failed across the ridge and verges, or if your roof covering is simply reaching the end of its natural lifespan, a re-roof is usually the most cost-effective solution in the long run. We'll give you an honest assessment and never push you towards a bigger job than you need.

Materials We Fit

We work with the materials that suit both your property and your budget. For traditional and period homes across Tayside and Angus, natural slate — including Scotch slate — is often the first choice: it's beautiful, breathes well, and lasts generations when fitted correctly. Clay and concrete tiles are an excellent, cost-effective alternative for many properties, offering durability and a wide range of profiles and colours. We'll walk you through what's right for your roof, your street and your pocket.

What a Storm Guard Re-Roof Includes

  • Full strip of the existing covering — old tiles, slates and felt removed and disposed of responsibly.
  • New breathable roofing membrane — modern underlay that allows moisture to escape and keeps the roof structure healthy.
  • Treated battens — properly sized and pressure-treated for long-term durability.
  • Re-lay of slate or tile — fitted to manufacturer specification and secured correctly for Scottish weather conditions.
  • Dry ridge & verge systems — mechanical fixing instead of mortar for a maintenance-free finish that won't crack or fail.
  • Fresh leadwork — new flashing around chimneys, valleys, abutments and any roof penetrations.
  • Optional insulation upgrade — a re-roof is the ideal time to improve your loft insulation and reduce heat loss.

Repair or Replace? Honest Advice

We never push a full re-roof when a repair will do the job. If your tiles are sound but your ridge mortar has failed, or you have a handful of slipped slates and a leaking valley, targeted repairs are usually the right call — faster, cheaper and with a good guarantee behind them. When we inspect your roof, we'll tell you exactly what we find and what we recommend, with no pressure either way. You can read more about our repair work on our roof repairs page.

Common Questions

New Roof FAQs

How long does a new roof take?

Most domestic re-roofs take a few days to a week, depending on the size of the roof, the materials and the weather. We'll give you a realistic timescale with your quote and keep you updated throughout.

How much does a new roof cost?

It depends on the size and pitch of your roof and the materials you choose. We give clear, fixed quotes after a free survey — no obligation, no pressure.

Should I choose slate or tile?

Both are excellent. Natural slate suits traditional and period properties and lasts generations; concrete or clay tiles are a cost-effective, durable choice for many homes. We'll talk you through what best suits your property and budget.

Do I really need a new roof, or just a repair?

Not every tired-looking roof needs replacing. We'll always tell you honestly — if a repair will do, we'll say so.
