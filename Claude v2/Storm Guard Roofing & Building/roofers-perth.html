Roofing in Perth

Roofers Who Know Perth's Roofs

Storm Guard Roofing & Building are trusted local roofers working right across Perth — from the period sandstone townhouses of the city centre and Bridgend to the suburbs of Craigie and Letham, and out towards Scone and the surrounding villages. Perth sits on the River Tay and serves as a gateway to the Highlands, and we're on hand to keep the roofs of the Fair City weather-tight all year round.

Perth's streets are a mix of elegant Georgian and Victorian architecture, conservation-area sandstone properties and modern housing estates. Older homes across the city commonly carry natural slate roofs, which need skilled, careful hands when it comes to repairs and re-roofing. We've worked across all of these roof types and know what each one demands.

Period and Conservation Properties in Perth

Perth has a wealth of Georgian and Victorian sandstone buildings, and many sit within conservation areas where the character of the roofline matters as much as the function. Natural slate is the traditional choice across these older streets, and we work to match it correctly — sourcing compatible slate, using traditional fixing methods and carrying out lime repointing where it's needed. Whether you have a listed building or simply a period sandstone property you want to look after properly, we'll give you honest advice and do the job right.

Perth's River Tay Weather and Storm Response

Perth's riverside setting is part of its character, but wind and rain off the Tay test roofs hard — particularly flashing, leadwork and chimney stacks on older properties. Even a short spell of rough weather can lift slates, crack pointing or shift ridge tiles that were already starting to loosen. We respond quickly to storm call-outs across the city and its surrounding villages, making your roof safe and watertight as the first priority, then talking you through any longer-term repair or replacement work at your convenience.
