Roofing in Aberdeen

Roofing Work in Aberdeen's Granite City

Storm Guard Roofing & Building are based in the Dundee and Angus area, and we extend our coverage north to Aberdeen for larger projects and for customers who already know our work. It's a longer run than our usual patch, but Aberdeen is a city we're happy to travel to when the job warrants it — and we bring the same standards with us.

Aberdeen is one of Scotland's most distinctive cities. Known as the Granite City, much of its built environment is constructed from locally quarried grey granite — from the grand civic buildings to the rows of traditional tenements. The majority of these properties carry natural slate roofs, and maintaining them properly calls for experience with traditional materials and methods. That's work we do regularly across our core Dundee and Angus area, so Aberdeen's housing stock is familiar territory.

Our Roofing Services in Aberdeen

  • New roofs — full tile & slate re-roofs built to last.
  • Roof repairs — slipped slates, leaks and storm damage addressed properly.
  • Leadwork — flashing, valleys and chimney soakers on traditional stone-built properties.
  • Chimney repairs — repointing, rebuilds and cowls on granite stacks.
  • Guttering and uPVC roofline — fascias, soffits and rainwater goods.
  • Repointing — ridges, verges and stonework repointed and sealed against damp.

Granite Buildings, Slate Roofs & Coastal Weather

Aberdeen sits on the north-east coast of Scotland, exposed to North Sea weather that few other cities have to contend with. Salt-laden winds, driving rain and sharp temperature swings put rooflines, flashings and ridge mortar under sustained pressure — and on older granite tenements, small defects can become costly problems quickly if they're left.

Natural slate holds up well when it's in good order, but aging fixings, cracked slates and failed leadwork are common on properties that haven't been properly maintained. We're experienced in identifying exactly what needs attention and dealing with it in a way that's appropriate for traditional construction — without over-specifying work that isn't needed.

Larger Projects and Existing Customers

Because of the distance from our Dundee base, Aberdeen works best for us when the job is substantial enough to make the journey worthwhile — a full re-roof, significant storm damage, or a planned programme of repairs on a larger property. We're also pleased to travel for existing customers who've moved to Aberdeen or have property there.

For smaller or more urgent jobs — a couple of slipped slates or a guttering fix — customers in our Angus towns like Montrose or Brechin can usually be reached far more quickly. If you're not sure whether your project suits us travelling to Aberdeen, just give us a call and we'll be straight with you.
